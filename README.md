# VLM Medical Survey 🏥

Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have recently emerged as a powerful paradigm for learning joint representations from visual and textual data. Their ability to integrate multimodal information has led to significant advancements in domains such as image understanding, natural language reasoning, and cross-modal retrieval.

In the medical domain, VLMs are increasingly being explored for applications such as radiology report generation, medical visual question answering (VQA), and clinical decision support. These models aim to bridge the gap between complex medical imaging data and textual clinical knowledge, enabling more interpretable and scalable AI systems for healthcare.

This repository presents a curated and structured collection of research papers focused on Vision-Language Models in medicine. The goal is to organize existing work, identify key research directions, and support the development of a comprehensive survey of this rapidly evolving field.

---

## 📌 Objective

* To explore Vision-Language Models in healthcare
* To categorize research based on tasks and models
* To build a structured understanding for a survey paper
